Atlantic Hurricane Intensity Dashboard (2013â€“2023)
An interactive dashboard analyzing Atlantic hurricane activity with a focus on storm intensity rather than just frequency.

Key questions explored:
Are hurricanes becoming more frequent?
Are hurricanes becoming stronger?
How does central pressure relate to wind speed?

Key insights:
Storm counts fluctuate year to year without a clear upward trend.
Average maximum wind speeds remain elevated in recent years.
A clear inverse relationship exists between central pressure and wind speed, reinforcing the physical mechanisms behind storm intensification.
Even without record storm counts, stronger storms increase risks of flooding, storm surge, and infrastructure damage.

Tools & methods:
NOAA HURDAT2 hurricane dataset
Data cleaning and aggregation
Looker Studio for visualization
Time-series and relationship analysis
